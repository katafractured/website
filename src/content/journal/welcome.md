This is the journal.

It's where I'll write about the platform underneath the apps — why we built it the way we did, what we tried that didn't work, what's coming next, and what I think about the broader privacy world when something happens that's worth thinking out loud about.

It's not a marketing blog. There won't be "10 best privacy tools in 2026" listicles. There won't be SEO-bait pages that exist to funnel you to a pricing page.

It's not a changelog. Routine version bumps live at [docs.katafract.io/changelog/](https://docs.katafract.io/changelog/). The journal is for the parts that earn the attention.

It's not a forum. There are no comments. If you want to discuss a post, reply to the newsletter, or post about it on Hacker News or your Mastodon instance — that's where the conversation happens.

The expectation is roughly one founder essay per month, plus the occasional architecture deep-dive or post-mortem when something warrants it. Quality over schedule. If a month is dry, the month is dry.

If this sounds like the kind of writing you'd want in your inbox, the newsletter goes out when posts ship. If you'd rather pull, the [RSS feed](/journal/feed.xml) is always live.

— Tek
