Getting Started

Wraith is a privacy-first VPN app built on WireGuard. It routes your internet traffic through one of our globally distributed servers, masking your IP address and encrypting everything between your device and the exit node. Haven DNS — ad and tracker blocking at the DNS layer — is included at every tier.
  1. Download Wraith from the App Store.
  2. Sign in with Apple. No email or password required.
  3. Subscribe to Haven (DNS only) or Enclave (full VPN) from the Settings tab.
  4. On the main screen, choose a server node or leave it on automatic.
  5. Tap Connect. iOS will ask you to authorize the VPN configuration once — tap Allow.
Haven — $1.99/mo ($14.99/yr): DNS-only protection. Haven DNS filtering on all our nodes. Blocks ads and trackers at the DNS layer. No VPN tunnel.

Enclave — $4.99/mo ($39.99/yr): WireGuard VPN with Haven DNS. Single tunnel, kill switch, tunnel mode selection, and access to global nodes.

Enclave+ — $9.99/mo ($79.99/yr): Everything in Enclave plus multi-hop double-encapsulated routing. Your traffic routes through two separate servers for enhanced privacy.
Wraith requires iOS 17.0 or later on iPhone. iPad support is coming. A macOS app is in development. Android is planned.

Connection & Tunnel Modes

Standard mode routes only DNS queries through Wraith. Your browsing traffic goes directly to its destination, but DNS lookups — which reveal every site you try to reach — are filtered through Haven. This offers strong privacy at maximum speed.

Full mode routes all your internet traffic through the Wraith server, replacing your IP address and encrypting everything end-to-end. Use Full mode when you need complete anonymity or are on an untrusted network (hotel WiFi, coffee shops, airports).
In the app, tap Settings (gear icon) and toggle between Standard and Full mode. Changes take effect immediately — the active connection will restart to apply the new configuration.
Tap the server name on the main screen to expand the node list. Available nodes are shown with their region and latency. Tap any node to select it, then tap Connect. Wraith auto-selects the nearest node if you leave it on Automatic.
Current nodes: Portland (US West), Ashburn (US East), Newark (US East-2), Nuremberg (EU West), Helsinki (EU North), Singapore (AP Southeast), Tokyo (AP Northeast), Mumbai (AP South). New nodes are added to the app automatically — no update required.
WireGuard connections are stateless and resume automatically when network changes (switching from WiFi to cellular, or vice versa). If the connection dropped and didn’t auto-recover, try toggling the connection off and back on. If the issue persists, check your iOS VPN configuration under Settings → General → VPN & Device Management and ensure Wraith’s configuration is present.

Haven DNS

Haven is our DNS filtering layer, running on every Wraith server node. When your device looks up a domain (which happens for every site you visit, every link you tap, every app that phones home), Haven checks it against curated blocklists. Ads, trackers, malware domains, and telemetry endpoints are blocked before a connection is ever made. No blocker extension required. No DNS queries sent to Google or your ISP.
Yes. Haven DNS is included with every plan, including the Haven-only tier. Enclave and Enclave+ subscribers get Haven filtering inside the VPN tunnel as well.
Haven uses the AdGuard DNS filter and OISD full blocklist, covering advertising networks, cross-site trackers, telemetry endpoints, social media tracking pixels, and known malware domains. Legitimate content and sites are never blocked.
Occasionally a legitimate domain shares infrastructure with an ad network and gets caught in a blocklist. If a site stops loading while Wraith is connected, try toggling the connection off temporarily to test. If that resolves it, email us at hello@katafract.com with the domain and we’ll investigate a whitelist.

Kill Switch

The kill switch blocks all internet traffic if the Wraith tunnel drops unexpectedly. Without it, iOS would fall back to your real connection — briefly exposing your real IP address and unencrypted traffic. With it active, your device goes offline until the tunnel is re-established. Nothing leaks.
The kill switch is available in Full tunnel mode only. Open Settings in the app and toggle Kill Switch. It is enabled by default for Full mode users. Standard (DNS-only) mode doesn’t route traffic through the tunnel, so the kill switch doesn’t apply.
If you’re in Full mode with kill switch enabled and the tunnel dropped, yes — that’s expected behavior. Open Wraith and tap Connect to re-establish the tunnel, which will restore internet access. Alternatively, toggle the kill switch off in Settings if you want connectivity to resume without the tunnel.

Account & Subscription

No traditional account is created. Sign in with Apple is the only sign-in method. Apple provides Wraith with an anonymized identifier that ties your subscription to your Apple ID — no email address or password is required or stored on our end.
Subscriptions purchased through the App Store are managed by Apple. Go to iOS Settings → [Your Name] → Subscriptions to upgrade, downgrade, or cancel. Cancellation stops renewal at the end of the current billing period — you retain access until then.
Sign in to Wraith with the same Apple ID used for the original purchase, then tap Restore Purchases in Settings. Your subscription will be recognized instantly at no charge.
Refunds for App Store purchases are handled by Apple at reportaproblem.apple.com. Katafract cannot issue refunds for in-app purchases. If you have a billing dispute, contact Apple Support directly.

Privacy & Logging

No. Wraith does not log connection timestamps, session duration, your real IP address, the IP addresses of sites you visit, or any DNS queries. Our servers have no record of what you did while connected. This is a deliberate architectural decision — not a policy we can be pressured to change after the fact.
The app collects: your anonymized Apple ID identifier (to validate subscription status), your active subscription tier, and aggregate non-identifying app telemetry (crash reports only, via Apple’s built-in system). No network activity data is collected at any point.
We can only hand over what exists. Connection logs, session records, and DNS query histories are not stored. An order compelling us to produce them would produce nothing, because there is nothing to produce. For the data we do hold (subscription status, anonymized Apple ID), we follow applicable law and would notify affected users when legally permitted to do so.
Subscription and account data is stored on servers in the EU (Netherlands) operated by Katafract LLC. VPN traffic is processed only in-memory on the node you connect to and is not persisted anywhere.

Troubleshooting

Try these steps in order:
  1. Check that you have an active subscription in Settings.
  2. Toggle Airplane mode on and off to reset network state.
  3. Force-quit Wraith and reopen it.
  4. Go to iOS Settings → General → VPN & Device Management and delete any existing Wraith VPN configuration, then reconnect from the app to recreate it.
  5. If nothing works, restart your iPhone.
Still not connecting? Email us with your iOS version and the node you were trying to reach.
Tap Restore Purchases in Settings to force a subscription check. If the issue persists after 60 seconds, sign out and back in with Apple. The app validates subscriptions against Apple’s servers — a brief network hiccup can cause a stale status to display.
Try switching to a node geographically closer to you. In Full tunnel mode, all traffic routes through the server, so distance matters. Standard mode is nearly zero-overhead since it only filters DNS. If speeds are consistently poor on a nearby node, email us — it may indicate a server-side issue we need to investigate.
Some services (banking apps, streaming services, gaming) actively block known VPN IP ranges. This is a policy decision by those services, not a Wraith bug. Options: switch to Standard tunnel mode (DNS only) so the service sees your real IP for the connection, or disconnect while using that specific service. We rotate IP addresses periodically to reduce blocking, but we can’t guarantee compatibility with services that actively target VPN detection.

Pull to refresh on the home screen or check your device’s internet connection. If the status remains unavailable for more than a few minutes, check katafract.com or email us.
